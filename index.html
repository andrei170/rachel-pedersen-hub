Questions

The Ones An Operator At Your Stage Asks

Short, direct answers. The longer ones we cover on the call.

Why would I run this with an outside operator instead of in-house?

Because the team you have is already running the business. SMU is fulfilling, the Clique is delivering, the Shop is shipping, the podcast is publishing. Adding a 90-day ladder build, a premium-tier launch, a book funnel, and a paid acquisition layer on top of an already-loaded ops team is how those projects either get postponed by a year or get launched at half the quality. Outside operator is faster, cleaner, and exits when production hits steady state. We hand the system to your team after the launch cycle, not before.

How much of my time does this need?

Roughly half a day to record the mid-tier SKU positioning content. One day to record the Authority Mastermind positioning. Calendar holds for Mastermind delivery in Lever 02. The rest is build team work plus you reviewing the copy, creative, and pricing decisions before they ship. Most operators at your stage spend under 8 hours a month during the 90-day build, and under 2 hours a month after the system is in production.

Will the Authority Mastermind cannibalise The Social Clique?

No. The Mastermind and the Clique solve different problems. The Clique is group coaching for operators learning to apply the social media playbook in their own business. The Mastermind is strategic operating-room access for operators who are already running their own social media business or signature offer and want to scale to authority-tier revenue. Different deliverable, different audience, different buying motion. Most Mastermind applicants will be Clique graduates moving up the ladder, not net-new buyers choosing Mastermind over Clique. Where there's overlap, the Mastermind is the explicit "post-Clique" tier. It expands the ladder, it doesn't compete inside it.

How does this work alongside my current team?

The build team operates alongside your existing structure. Your content team keeps producing. Your customer success team keeps onboarding Clique members. Your podcast and social cadence stays where it is. We layer on top: mid-tier SKU, SMU ascension flow, Mastermind sales engine, book funnel, paid layer, alumni community. The only meaningful interface point is Rachel on camera for the positioning content. Everything else, we run autonomously and report back.

What if my existing Social Clique already covers some of what the Mastermind would?

There's overlap on some content (positioning, content systems, client acquisition), but the deliverable is different. The Clique is "we coach you in a group setting alongside other students." The Mastermind is "you already run the business, here's how to operate at authority scale, with peer operators, in a strategic format that group coaching can't deliver." Most Mastermind attendees will have already passed through the Clique and outgrown the group coaching format. The overlap is feature, not bug. The Mastermind buys insider continuity for operators who don't want to start from scratch with another coach.

How quickly is there real revenue?

Lever 01 mid-tier SKU: first checkouts in week 4-6 once the SKU and the page ship. SMU-to-paid attach rate: measurable in week 5-7 once the segmented welcome flows start cycling. Lever 02 first Mastermind sells in month 2-3, runs in month 3-4. Lever 03 book funnel and paid layer start producing measurable ROAS by month 4. Lever 04 alumni community ramps from month 4 onward. Most of the year-one incremental lands in months 4-12, not month one.

What if the Mastermind doesn't fill?

The Mastermind is sized at 6-8 seats first, sold to existing Clique alumni and the warmest segment of the SMU free list (the warmest possible audience for this offer that exists). If the first intake under-fills (say, 4 confirmed instead of 6), we don't push it into a half-empty room. We pause, sharpen the positioning and the application screen, push the date by 30-45 days, and try again with the second wave of alumni outreach plus any external warm sources we can verify. Build doesn't continue billing while the Mastermind isn't running. If after a second attempt the audience isn't there at the modelled price, we restructure the tier or move the budget into Lever 03 paid acquisition.

What if I want to stop after a Lever?

You can stop at any Lever. After Lever 01 you own the mid-tier SKU, the SMU ascension flow, and the segmented nurture as standalone lifts on your existing funnel. After Lever 02 you own the Mastermind sales page, application flow, 90-day curriculum, and the first cohort revenue. After Lever 03 you own the book funnel and the paid acquisition system. Each Lever stands on its own as a fully shipped asset in case the rest stops being interesting to you.
